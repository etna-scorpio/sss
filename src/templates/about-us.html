I was educated at Methodist College Belfast and graduated from Queen's University, Belfast with an Honours Degree in Law in 1984 and a year later from the Institute of Professional Legal Studies with a Certificate in Professional Legal Studies. I also hold a Law Society Certificate in Advanced Advocacy, with which I appear and represent clients at many Courts within the province. I am also a member of the Advocacy Working Party of the Law Society of Northern Ireland and was until recently an elected member of the Council of the Law Society of Northern Ireland.

Admitted as a solicitor to the Supreme Court of Jurisdiction in Northern Ireland in 1985, I joined the well-reputed Johnsons Solicitors of Belfast. After gaining extensive experience in a wide range of legal matters from general property matters, such as the buying and selling of houses to the administration of estates, I moved quickly into dealing with personal injury actions and commercial Court cases.

I was approached by Thompsons solicitors in Newtownards and for the purposes of career advancement moved to the firm in 1987 where I was made partner two years later. In Thompsons, I was responsible for all court matters including personal injury claims for individual claimants, commercial and business disputes some matrimonial and various property transactions and clinical negligence cases. I gained extensive experience and expertise.

Since I founded Stephen Scott and Company solicitors in Newtownards in 1998, I have established further expertise, dealing personally with property related matters, commercial related matters, personal injury cases and commercial cases. I also assist in the matrimonial side of the practice. I am also a member of a number of Court Committees that regulate court procedure in the High Court and County Court in Northern Ireland.
